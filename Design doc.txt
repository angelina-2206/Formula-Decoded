Formula Decoded
Design Document

Tagline: Breaking Down the Technology of Modern Formula One

1. Design Vision

Formula Decoded will be a cinematic, interactive web experience that visually explains the engineering of modern Formula One cars.

The design approach combines:

3D technical visualization
scroll-based storytelling
interactive engineering diagrams
modern motorsport aesthetics

The experience should feel similar to:

a Formula One engineering lab
an Apple-style product launch page
an interactive motorsport exhibit

Users should feel like they are exploring the anatomy of an F1 car rather than browsing a traditional website.

2. Visual Inspiration

The design direction is inspired by:

Technical F1 illustrations
Interactive motorsport dashboards
High-contrast UI systems
Engineering blueprints

Key inspiration themes from the references you shared:

1. Technical blueprint style

Layered diagrams showing the internal structure of the car.

2. Minimal motorsport UI

Dark backgrounds with bright accents and glowing highlights.

3. Interactive product storytelling

Scrolling reveals new sections of the car.

6. Scroll-Driven Storytelling

The entire website is structured around scroll-based narrative sections.

Each scroll section introduces one engineering concept.

Section 1 — Explore the Car

User scrolls.

The car rotates slowly.

Hotspots appear on the car.

Example hotspots:

Front Wing
Halo
Sidepods
Power Unit
Rear Wing
Diffuser

Hovering highlights components.

Clicking zooms into the part.

Section 2 — Aerodynamics

Focus on the active aerodynamic system introduced in the new regulations.

Active aero replaces DRS and allows front and rear wings to adjust between straight mode (low drag) and corner mode (high downforce).

Scroll animation:

wings expand
airflow particles appear
drag reduces

Particle simulation shows airflow around the car.

Section 3 — Hybrid Power Unit

The next section explains the hybrid energy system.

The 2026 power units will split power roughly 50-50 between the combustion engine and electric system.

Visual interaction:

energy flows through the car

battery level animations

power delivery diagrams

Modes to show:

Normal Mode
Overtake Mode
Recharge Mode
Section 4 — Active Aero Interaction

User can toggle modes.

Example UI controls:

Corner Mode
Straight Mode
Overtake Mode

When switching modes:

front wing angle changes

rear wing opens

airflow patterns update

Active aerodynamics allow wings to move between low-drag and high-downforce configurations depending on track conditions.

Section 5 — Exploded Engineering View

Scroll transition.

The car breaks apart into components.

Parts float outward in an exploded diagram.

Components visible:

chassis
engine
battery
suspension
gearbox
wings

Hovering shows technical explanations.

Section 6 — Driver Grid

Next section introduces the current drivers.

Animated cards.

Each card contains:

driver photo

team

nationality

stats

Cards expand when clicked.

Section 7 — Race Calendar

Interactive world map.

Tracks appear as glowing nodes.

Hovering reveals race details.

Example tracks:

Monaco

Silverstone

Monza

Section 8 — History of Formula One

A scroll timeline showing major eras.

Sections include:

1950s early racing
Turbo era
V10 era
Hybrid era
2026 future era

Cars change visually as the timeline progresses.
